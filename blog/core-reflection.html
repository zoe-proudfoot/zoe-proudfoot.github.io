Core Reflection

Sprint One

Thinking Like a Web Developer:

Thinking like a web developer involves approaching problems analytically, breaking them down into smaller, manageable parts, and devising efficient and logical solutions. As a newcomer to development, I initially faced some challenges in adopting this mindset. However, over time, I have made significant progress and look forward to further refining these skills throughout my time in the boot camp

Identity and Values Exploration:

During the Foundations course, I engaged in extensive self-reflection, which helped reawaken my dormant creative side. Crafting blogs and designing them using CSS brought me immense satisfaction. Additionally, I had ample opportunities to discuss my experiences with autism, something I rarely get to do. It felt liberating to open up about it and address the associated struggles.

Introduce Yourself Exercise:

At first, I hesitated and almost decided against participating in the 'Introduce Yourself' exercise. Nonetheless, completing it provided a sense of relief and accomplishment. Although it remains a personal challenge, I recognize that it's an area where I can continually work on.

Sprint Two

My Learning Plan:

I didn't start with a detailed learning plan as I tend to prefer a more flexible approach, adapting to circumstances as they arise. However, I have set a long-term goal of securing a good job upon completion of this course. I firmly believe that I'm still on track to achieve that objective.

Sprint Four

Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset:

Exploring the concept of neuroplasticity and embracing a growth mindset has been one of the highlights of the core research. I genuinely believe that I have a growth mindset as I find joy in acquiring new knowledge and remain open to new experiences. Learning is an ongoing process that allows us to continue growing even after our physical growth has ceased. I eagerly anticipate further learning and personal growth during my time at Dev Academy.

Listening and Looping:

I found the listening part of this exercise relatively easy because I naturally possess good listening skills. However, I encountered difficulties when it came to speaking. I found it challenging and often stumbled over my words. Nevertheless, I gave it my best effort, and as a result, I can now reflect on the experience and articulate it effectively.
