The Problem Solving Blog

This is my problem-solving blog. I found myself stuck more times than I would care to admit. But I would always find ways to solve 'em. Here's a few examples.

A time I was blocked on a simple problem:

A lot of the setting up process at the start of Foundations was full of blockages for me. The first one was waiting on my laptop to arrive so that I could start working. I remember the day I was setting up Visual Studio Code (where i make the cool stuff happen 😉) in particular. The settings checker in the terminal was missing a lot and I felt myself getting frustrated because it wasn't doing what I wanted it to do. What I did that day was I stepped away from the computer for a while and had a break. When I came back to it with a fresh mind, I was able to troubleshoot and fix the issue. 🤘

When I "Elegantly" solved a problem:

I'm gonna fess and say I'm not a very "Elegant" person. in fact, I get through most of my issues with a lot of awkward. But that's the Zoe Proudfoot charm. But I suppose there were many instances where I felt myself wanting to launch the computer down the street. But, like I said before, I walk away and take a short break most of the times where I'm frustrated instead of damaging things. But I suppose for the times I have found myself getting stuck and needing a quick tip, I would visit Google for answers. I have a feeling Google is going to become my bestie over the rest of this course. But if Google wasn't helping, I would as ChatGPT for some hints and most of the time, it came through. But if I got really, REALLY stuck, I would go to the #tech-questions on the Discord Server and ask my fellows for some help.

How confident was I with these methods?

I felt confident with these methods, and most of the time, I was able to get out of these tough spots.

A time where I was reluctant to ask for help:

I think the real question should be when am I not reluctant to ask for help. I don't know if that's due to my anxiety or other factors, but I've always been somewhat reluctant to ask for help. But over these past weeks, I have felt myself getting more comfortable in asking for help when I've needed it. I just have to remind myself that I am learning and that it is OK to ask for help when I need it.
