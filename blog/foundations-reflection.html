What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

Through my core learning experience, I've encountered three new aspects about myself and my ego. Firstly, I realized that I tend to underestimate my abilities and achievements. While I value humility, I acknowledge the importance of recognizing and giving myself credit when it is deserved.

Secondly, I've found that despite having autism and sometimes struggling with social cues, I posess emotional intellegce and the ability to be empathetic. This enables me to provide valuble support to the important people in my life.

Lastly, I've come to realize that I thrive on routine, and I've successfully established a beneficial one during Foundations. However, I recognize the need to start my activities earlier to allow for more study time. I've discovered that my optimal focus hours begin around 10 AM when my mind is clear and funtioning at its best.

Values, empathy, and self awareness are crucial in learning and programming. Values guide ethical choices, empathy fosters user understanding, and self-awareness enhances decision-making and personal growth.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

We prioritize core learning in our web development Bootcamp course because we recognize the importance of interpersonal skills alongside technical expertise. In any industry, including programming, the ability to effectively interact with others is paramount. Developers frequently collaborate with colleagues, work for clients, and engage in team projects. It is crucial to cultivate positive relationships and demonstrate professionalism. Even if one excels in website development, a negative attitude or disrespectful behavior can severely hinder professional success. By emphasizing core learning, we aim to equip our students with the essential skills to communicate effectively, collaborate efficiently, and build strong working relationships. This holistic approach enhances career prospects, fosters positive team dynamics, and contributes to overall professional growth.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, I do not believe that the time spent studying core learning was a waste of time. While it is true that dedicating more time to programming practice could have been beneficial, the core learning components offered valuable insights and benefits that complement programming skills.
By engaging with emotional intelligence and neuroplasticity exercises, I gained a deeper understanding of myself and how I can improve as a programmer. These learnings helped me recognize the significance of people skills in the field of programming. Developing strong interpersonal abilities, such as effective communication and empathy, is essential for collaboration, teamwork, and client interactions.
Furthermore, core learning provided a holistic approach to my development as a professional. It expanded my perspective beyond coding and technical aspects, emphasizing the importance of personal growth, self-awareness, and professionalism. These elements contribute to long-term success and satisfaction in the industry.
In summary, while more programming practice is valuable, the time invested in core learning was well-spent as it provided invaluable insights, enhanced self-awareness, and emphasized the importance of people skills in the programming field.
