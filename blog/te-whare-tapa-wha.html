4 Dimensions of Wellbeing

  • Taha tinana (physical wellbeing)
  • Taha hinengaro (mental wellbeing)
  • Taha wairua (spiritual wellbeing)
  • Taha wh훮nau (family wellbeing)

How am I going to put these into practice?

Taha Tinana (Pysical Wellbeing)

I have started to make a habit of walking my dog on any day that it's not raining. I suppose I will carry that on for my physical wellbeing. The exercise is also mutually beneficial for the two of us. I do need to work on my sleep schedule, in terms of getting my 8 hours.

Taha Hinegaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

Practicing self-care. I will engage in relaxing activities, such as reading, listening to music and all of that good stuff. I will also journal/write down when I am really stressed. I will also try and find a good work/life balance. Also, despite how many times I've said it in the past, I will seek help if I need to.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

Connecting with nature. I believe walks along our beautiful Gizzy beaches and such would be great. Cultivate gratitude. I have a habit of stopping to appreciate the small things in life, so I will carry on with that. Altruism and helping others. I suppose I should have put this in my intro blog, but I have done many hours of voulentary work. At the Riding For the Disabled, and also at the SPCA. I suppose, I could help people when I have the mental capacity and the time to do so, because it does make me feel good.

Taha Wh훮nau (family wellbeing)

Healthy boundaries. I suppose this is moreso for myself as I have always been a yes person to avoid conflict. So I guess, I need to learn to say no a little more. Emotional support. Be there to support my family emotionally. And communicate effectively. I don't have an explination for that, but I see its importance.

Whenua (land)

I will take steps to conserve natural resources and look after my environment. I will also take whatever opportunity I can to connect with nature.
